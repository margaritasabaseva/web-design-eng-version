Dzelzavas Street

To get to Dzelzavas Street, I walk through the labyrinth of apartment buildings on Džohara Dudajeva Street and cross the so-called Kremlin wall aka the wall of nine-storey buildings, which stretches along Dzelzavas Street from Gunāra Astras to Ilūkstes Street. Trolleybuses start their way on this street, the destination of which is called "Purvciems" – those are trolleybuses no.17 and 23. I'm going to the nearest stop - "Mēbeļu nams", which is named after the building across the street where the company of the same name is located.

The stop of the "Mēbeļu nams" is located at the wide intersection of Dzelzavas Street and Gunāra Astras Street, where in each of the four directions there are roads with at least two lanes in each direction. After boarding one of the two mentioned trolleybuses, the road continues along Dzelzavas Street with the help of public transport, during which it passes the shopping center "Minska", until the end of Vaidavas Street, where the paths of both trolleybus routes are further separated. However, the final destination for them is the same as for me – the center of Riga.
