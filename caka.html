Aleksandra Čaka iela

As mentioned above, the Zemitānu bridge connects Ieriķu Street with Aleksandra Čaka Street. As the name implies, it was named in honor of the famous Latvian poet Aleksandrs Čaks. When the weather is warmer outside, I like to get off a few stops before the final stop in the center and visit the Ziedoņdārzs Park between A. Čaka, Matīsa and Artilērijas streets, where a monument was erected in 1981 in honor of the same Čaka.

Also near Ziedoņdārzs Park is the “Sporta kvartāls” of the center of Riga, which is popular with people who enojoy various sports. I am very pleased that Riga continues to improve and implement such projects. It should also be mentioned that the road infrastructure on A. Čaka Street itself has recently been completely renovated.
